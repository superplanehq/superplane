# PRD Writing

Use this skill to create or review PRDs in this repository.

## Default approach
- Keep PRDs concise and implementation-oriented.
- Prefer concrete bullet points over long prose.
- Include only information needed to decide and deliver.

## Required sections
1. **Problem Statement** (what is missing/broken and why now)
2. **Goals** (desired outcomes)
3. **Non-Goals** (explicitly out of scope)
4. **Users / Personas** (who benefits or is affected)
5. **Success Metrics** (measurable outcomes)
6. **Product Requirements** (functional and non-functional requirements)
7. **Risks & Open Questions** (unknowns, assumptions, dependencies)

## Quality checks
- Every requirement is testable.
- Every goal maps to at least one metric.
- Non-goals clearly prevent scope creep.
- Line length is always limited to 120 characters.

## What to avoid
- Adding a **Document Control** section.
